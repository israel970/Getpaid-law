Understanding Premises Liability Cases

Premises liability holds property owners responsible for injuries that occur on their property due to dangerous conditions. This includes slip and falls, inadequate security, swimming pool accidents, and more.

Property owners have a duty to maintain safe conditions or warn visitors of known hazards. When they fail, victims can seek compensation for their injuries.

Types of Premises Liability Cases

Slip and Fall

Wet floors, uneven surfaces, and other hazards causing falls.

Inadequate Security

Assaults and crimes due to lack of proper security measures.

Swimming Pool Accidents

Drowning and injuries at pools without proper safeguards.

Dog Bites

Attacks by dogs on property where the owner knew of dangerous behavior.

Elevator Accidents

Injuries from malfunctioning or improperly maintained elevators.

Parking Lot Injuries

Poor lighting, potholes, and other hazards in parking areas.

Compensation for Premises Liability Victims

Victims may be entitled to substantial compensation including:

Medical Expenses

Emergency care, surgeries, rehabilitation, and ongoing treatment costs

Lost Income

Wages lost during recovery and reduced future earning capacity

Pain & Suffering

Physical pain, emotional distress, and loss of quality of life

Future Care Costs

Long-term medical needs, therapy, and assistive devices

Frequently Asked Questions

What must I prove in a premises liability case?

You must prove the owner knew or should have known of the hazard, failed to fix or warn about it, and this caused your injury.

What if I was trespassing?

Property owners owe limited duties to trespassers, but may still be liable if they act with willful or wanton disregard for safety.

Can I sue a business for criminal acts of third parties?

Yes, if the business knew or should have known of security risks and failed to provide adequate protection.

What damages can I recover?

Victims can recover medical expenses, lost wages, pain and suffering, and in some cases punitive damages.
