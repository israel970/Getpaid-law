Why You Need a Concrete Mixer Accident Lawyer

Concrete mixer trucks, also known as cement trucks, are among the most dangerous vehicles on the road. With their constantly rotating drums and high center of gravity, these trucks are prone to rollovers and can weigh over 70,000 pounds when fully loaded.

These accidents often occur near construction sites and involve complex liability questions between trucking companies, construction contractors, and equipment manufacturers. Our attorneys have experience navigating these multi-party claims.

Types of Concrete Mixer Accident Cases

Rollover Accidents

Top-heavy concrete mixers are prone to tipping over on curves and uneven surfaces.

Construction Zone Crashes

Accidents involving cement trucks entering and exiting active construction sites.

Rear-End Collisions

Heavy cement trucks cannot stop quickly and cause devastating rear-end crashes.

Spill Accidents

Concrete spills creating road hazards and causing multi-vehicle accidents.

Equipment Failures

Rotating drum malfunctions and other mechanical failures causing accidents.

Worker Injuries

Construction workers struck or crushed by cement trucks on job sites.

Proving Liability in Concrete Mixer Accident Cases

Establishing liability in truck accident cases requires thorough investigation and understanding of federal trucking regulations. Key elements we examine include:

  • Driver Negligence: Speeding, distracted driving, fatigue, or impairment
  • Company Negligence: Inadequate training, pressure to violate hours of service, poor maintenance
  • Regulatory Violations: FMCSA violations, overweight loads, improper cargo securement
  • Equipment Failures: Defective brakes, tires, or other mechanical components

Frequently Asked Questions

Why are concrete mixer accidents so dangerous?

These trucks combine extreme weight with a high center of gravity, making them prone to rollovers. The rotating drum also affects handling and braking.

Who is liable for a concrete mixer accident?

Potentially liable parties include the truck driver, trucking company, concrete supplier, construction company, and truck manufacturer.

What if the accident happened in a construction zone?

Construction zone accidents may involve additional liable parties and require investigation of site safety protocols.

How much time do I have to file a claim?

Statutes of limitations vary by state, but you should contact an attorney immediately to preserve evidence and protect your rights.
