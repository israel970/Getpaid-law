Understanding Medical Malpractice Cases

Medical malpractice occurs when healthcare providers fail to meet the accepted standard of care, resulting in patient injury or death. These cases include surgical errors, misdiagnosis, medication mistakes, and birth injuries.

Medical malpractice cases are complex and require expert medical testimony. Our attorneys work with medical experts to prove negligence and fight for maximum compensation.

Types of Medical Malpractice Cases

Surgical Errors

Wrong-site surgery, retained surgical instruments, and anesthesia errors.

Misdiagnosis

Failure to diagnose cancer, heart attacks, strokes, and other serious conditions.

Medication Errors

Wrong medication, wrong dosage, and dangerous drug interactions.

Birth Injuries

Cerebral palsy, brachial plexus injuries, and other injuries during delivery.

Hospital Negligence

Infections, falls, understaffing, and equipment failures.

Emergency Room Errors

Failure to properly triage, diagnose, or treat emergency patients.

Compensation for Medical Malpractice Victims

Victims may be entitled to substantial compensation including:

Medical Expenses

Emergency care, surgeries, rehabilitation, and ongoing treatment costs

Lost Income

Wages lost during recovery and reduced future earning capacity

Pain & Suffering

Physical pain, emotional distress, and loss of quality of life

Future Care Costs

Long-term medical needs, therapy, and assistive devices

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the standard of care in medical malpractice?

The standard of care is what a reasonably competent healthcare provider in the same specialty would do under similar circumstances.

Do I need an expert witness?

Yes, medical malpractice cases almost always require testimony from a medical expert to establish the standard of care and how it was breached.

What is the statute of limitations for medical malpractice?

Deadlines vary by state, typically 2-3 years from the injury or when it was discovered. Contact an attorney immediately.

What damages can I recover?

You may recover medical expenses, lost wages, pain and suffering, disability, and in death cases, wrongful death damages.
