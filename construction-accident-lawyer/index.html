Why You Need a Construction Accident Lawyer

Construction sites are among the most dangerous workplaces in America. Workers face risks from heights, heavy machinery, electrical hazards, and collapsing structures every day. When safety protocols are ignored, serious injuries and deaths occur.

If you were injured on a construction site, you may have claims against multiple parties beyond workers compensation. General contractors, subcontractors, equipment manufacturers, and property owners can all be held liable for unsafe conditions.

Types of Construction Accident Cases

Scaffold Accidents

Falls from scaffolding, scaffold collapses, and being struck by falling scaffold materials.

Crane Accidents

Crane collapses, struck-by incidents, and accidents during lifting operations.

Trench Collapses

Cave-ins and trench collapses due to inadequate shoring or protective systems.

Falling Objects

Workers struck by tools, materials, or debris falling from heights.

Power Tool Injuries

Severe cuts, amputations, and injuries from saws, nail guns, and other power tools.

Structural Collapses

Building collapses during construction or demolition operations.

Proving Liability in Construction Accident Cases

Establishing liability in workplace injury cases requires thorough investigation and understanding of OSHA regulations and industry standards. Key elements we examine include:

  • Employer Negligence: Failure to provide safe working conditions, proper training, or required safety equipment
  • OSHA Violations: Documented safety violations that contributed to your injury
  • Equipment Defects: Faulty machinery or tools that caused or contributed to the accident
  • Third-Party Liability: Contractors, property owners, or manufacturers who share responsibility

Compensation for Construction Accident Victims

Workplace injury victims may be entitled to compensation beyond workers compensation including:

Medical Expenses

Emergency care, surgeries, rehabilitation, and ongoing treatment costs

Lost Income

Current and future lost wages, reduced earning capacity

Pain & Suffering

Physical pain, emotional distress, loss of enjoyment of life

Permanent Disability

Compensation for lasting impairments affecting your ability to work and live

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I sue my employer for a construction accident?

While workers comp generally bars suing your employer, you can sue third parties like general contractors, subcontractors, equipment manufacturers, and property owners.

What is OSHA and how does it affect my case?

OSHA sets safety standards for construction sites. Violations can be powerful evidence of negligence in your case.

What compensation can I recover?

Beyond workers comp, third-party claims can recover pain and suffering, full lost wages, and punitive damages in egregious cases.

How long do I have to file a construction accident claim?

Statutes of limitations vary by state and type of claim. Contact an attorney immediately to protect your rights.
