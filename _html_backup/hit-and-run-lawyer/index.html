Why You Need a Hit and Run Accident Lawyer

Hit and run accidents are traumatizing—you're injured, your vehicle is damaged, and the responsible driver fled the scene. Every year, over 737,000 hit and run crashes occur in the United States, leaving victims wondering how they'll pay their medical bills and repair their vehicles.

The good news: you likely have more options than you realize. Uninsured motorist coverage, underinsured motorist coverage, and other insurance options may provide compensation. And if the driver is found, you can pursue them directly.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I get compensation if the hit and run driver is never found?
Yes. Your own uninsured motorist (UM) coverage can pay for your injuries and damages even if the driver is never identified. Texas requires insurers to offer UM coverage, and most policies include it.
What should I do after a hit and run?
Call 911 immediately, try to note the vehicle description and license plate, look for witnesses, check for surveillance cameras nearby, seek medical attention, and file a police report. Contact your insurance company and a hit and run lawyer.
Is hit and run a felony in Texas?
Hit and run can be a felony in Texas if the accident caused serious injury or death. Leaving the scene of an accident with injuries is a third-degree felony, punishable by 2-10 years in prison.
How long do police investigate hit and runs?
Investigation timelines vary. Cases with clear evidence (video footage, witnesses, license plates) may be solved quickly. Cases without leads may remain open indefinitely. An attorney can help push for investigation progress.
