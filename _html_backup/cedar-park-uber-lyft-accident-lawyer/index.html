Texas Uber/Lyft Accident Laws

Understanding Texas law is crucial for maximizing your recovery. Here are key legal principles affecting uber/lyft accident cases:

  • TNC Insurance Requirements: Texas requires Transportation Network Companies like Uber/Lyft to maintain specific insurance coverage at different driver status levels.
  • Period 1 Coverage: When the driver is logged into the app but hasn't accepted a ride: $50,000/$100,000/$25,000 minimum coverage.
  • Period 2 & 3 Coverage: Once a ride is accepted through trip completion: $1 million liability coverage plus uninsured/underinsured motorist coverage.
  • Personal Insurance Gap: Most personal auto policies exclude rideshare driving. Drivers without rideshare endorsements may have coverage gaps.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cedar Park Uber/Lyft Accidents

How much insurance covers Uber/Lyft accidents?

Coverage depends on driver status. During active trips, up to $1 million in liability coverage applies. When logged in but waiting for rides, minimum coverage is $50,000/$100,000/$25,000. When offline, only the driver's personal insurance applies.

Can I sue Uber or Lyft directly?

Generally no—drivers are classified as independent contractors, not employees. However, you can pursue claims against the rideshare companies' insurance policies, which provide substantial coverage during active rides.

I was an Uber passenger injured in a crash. What are my options?

As a passenger, you're covered by Uber's $1 million insurance policy regardless of who caused the accident. You can also pursue claims against any at-fault drivers' insurance. Our attorneys ensure you maximize recovery from all available sources.

What if the Uber driver was at fault for my accident?

If the Uber driver caused the accident while on an active trip, the $1 million liability policy covers your injuries. If they were between rides or offline, coverage determination becomes more complex.

Where do most rideshare accidents occur in Cedar Park?

Rideshare accidents in Cedar Park commonly occur in entertainment districts, near the airport, around downtown hotels, and in busy commercial areas where drivers are frequently picking up and dropping off passengers.

Which hospitals should I go to after a rideshare accident in Cedar Park?

For serious injuries, seek trauma center care immediately. Cedar Park Regional Medical Center provides emergency services for accident victims. Document all medical treatment carefully—rideshare insurance claims require thorough documentation.

How long do Uber/Lyft accident claims take to resolve?

Rideshare claims can take 6-18 months due to coverage disputes and the complexity of determining which insurance applies. Having an experienced attorney often speeds the process by cutting through insurance company delays.

The rideshare driver was uninsured. Do I have options?

Yes. Uber and Lyft provide uninsured/underinsured motorist coverage during active trips. If you were injured by an uninsured rideshare driver during a trip, this coverage protects you.
