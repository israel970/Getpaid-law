Understanding Product Liability Cases

Product liability law holds manufacturers, distributors, and retailers responsible for injuries caused by defective products. This includes design defects, manufacturing defects, and failure to warn of dangers.

When companies put profits over safety and release dangerous products, victims deserve full compensation. Our attorneys take on major corporations to protect consumers.

Types of Product Liability Cases

Defective Vehicles

Airbag failures, brake defects, rollover-prone designs, and fuel system fires.

Dangerous Drugs

Medications with undisclosed side effects and dangerous drug interactions.

Medical Device Defects

Hip implants, pacemakers, and other devices that fail or cause injury.

Defective Appliances

Fires and injuries from defective stoves, heaters, and electrical products.

Childrens Products

Toys, cribs, and other products that injure children.

Industrial Equipment

Workplace injuries from defective machinery and tools.

Compensation for Product Liability Victims

Victims may be entitled to substantial compensation including:

Medical Expenses

Emergency care, surgeries, rehabilitation, and ongoing treatment costs

Lost Income

Wages lost during recovery and reduced future earning capacity

Pain & Suffering

Physical pain, emotional distress, and loss of quality of life

Future Care Costs

Long-term medical needs, therapy, and assistive devices

Frequently Asked Questions

What types of product defects are there?

Design defects (inherently dangerous designs), manufacturing defects (errors in production), and warning defects (failure to warn of dangers).

Do I need to prove the company was negligent?

Not always. Strict liability means manufacturers can be liable even without proof of negligence if the product was defective.

What if I was misusing the product?

Manufacturers must anticipate reasonably foreseeable misuse. Obvious misuse may limit recovery, but not always bar it.

What damages can I recover?

Medical expenses, lost wages, pain and suffering, and in cases of willful misconduct, punitive damages to punish the manufacturer.
