Understanding Your Neck Injury Case

Neck injuries are extremely common in car accidents, falls, and workplace incidents. From whiplash to cervical disc injuries, neck problems can cause debilitating pain, headaches, and limited range of motion. Insurance companies often minimize neck injury claims, calling them 'soft tissue injuries.' Our attorneys know how to prove the severity of your neck injury and fight for full compensation.

Types of Neck Injury Cases We Handle

Our network of attorneys has experience with all types of neck injury cases.

Whiplash

Rapid back-and-forth movement of the neck causing soft tissue damage.

Cervical Disc Herniation

Disc material in the neck pressing on spinal nerves.

Cervical Fracture

Broken bones in the neck vertebrae from trauma.

Cervical Sprain/Strain

Damage to neck muscles, tendons, or ligaments.

Pinched Nerve

Compressed nerve in the cervical spine causing pain and numbness.

Cervical Stenosis

Narrowing of the spinal canal in the neck area.

Frequently Asked Questions

How serious is a neck injury from a car accident?
Neck injuries range from minor strains that heal in weeks to severe injuries causing permanent disability. Even 'minor' whiplash can cause chronic pain lasting months or years. Any neck injury should be evaluated by a doctor.
What are symptoms of a neck injury?
Common symptoms include neck pain and stiffness, headaches, shoulder pain, arm numbness or tingling, dizziness, limited range of motion, and muscle spasms. Some symptoms may not appear for 24-72 hours after an accident.
How much compensation can I get for a neck injury?
Neck injury settlements vary widely. Minor whiplash cases may settle for $10,000-$30,000, while severe injuries requiring surgery or causing permanent problems can exceed $500,000.
Should I see a doctor after a car accident even if my neck feels fine?
Yes. Neck injuries often have delayed symptoms. Getting examined promptly creates medical documentation and ensures any injury is identified early when treatment is most effective.
