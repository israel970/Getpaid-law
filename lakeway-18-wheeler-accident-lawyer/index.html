Common Causes of 18-Wheeler Accidents

Driver Fatigue

Despite hours-of-service regulations, many truckers are pressured to drive beyond safe limits, falling asleep at the wheel.

Overloaded Cargo

Exceeding weight limits affects braking distance and vehicle control. Improperly secured cargo can shift, causing loss of control.

Brake Failures

18-wheelers require extensive braking systems. Inadequate maintenance leads to catastrophic brake failures on steep grades.

Tire Blowouts

Worn tires on heavy trucks can explode at highway speeds, sending debris across lanes and causing drivers to lose control.

Jackknife Accidents

When the trailer swings perpendicular to the cab, it can sweep across multiple lanes, striking numerous vehicles.

Wide Turn Accidents

18-wheelers need extra room to turn, often swinging wide and crushing vehicles in adjacent lanes.

Texas 18-Wheeler Accident Laws

Understanding Texas law is crucial for maximizing your recovery. Here are key legal principles affecting 18-wheeler accident cases:

  • Federal Weight Limits: 18-wheelers are limited to 80,000 pounds gross weight and specific axle weight limits. Overweight trucks have longer stopping distances and are more prone to brake failure.
  • CDL Requirements: 18-wheeler drivers must hold a Commercial Driver's License with specific endorsements. Employers must verify qualifications and cannot hire drivers with disqualifying violations.
  • Drug and Alcohol Testing: Federal law requires pre-employment, random, post-accident, and reasonable suspicion drug and alcohol testing for commercial drivers.
  • Electronic Logging Devices: Federal law requires 18-wheelers to use ELDs that automatically record driving time, preventing falsification of paper logs.

Frequently Asked Questions About Lakeway 18-Wheeler Accidents

What makes 18-wheeler accidents so dangerous?

The massive weight differential—80,000 pounds versus 4,000 pounds for a typical car—creates devastating forces. 18-wheelers also have much longer stopping distances, larger blind spots, and higher centers of gravity making rollovers more common.

How much compensation can I get for an 18-wheeler accident?

18-wheeler accident settlements are among the largest personal injury awards due to severe injuries and high insurance minimums ($750,000 for most interstate trucks). Settlements ranging from hundreds of thousands to several million dollars are common for serious injuries.

Who can I sue after an 18-wheeler accident in Lakeway?

Potentially liable parties include the driver, trucking company, cargo loading company, maintenance provider, truck or trailer manufacturer, and sometimes the shipper. We investigate thoroughly to identify all parties with responsibility and insurance coverage.

What should I do immediately after an 18-wheeler accident?

Call 911, seek medical attention, document the scene if possible, get the truck's DOT number and company name, and contact an attorney before speaking with any insurance company. Don't sign anything or give recorded statements.

Where do 18-wheeler accidents occur most often in Lakeway?

In Lakeway, 18-wheeler accident accidents frequently occur on RM 620 (particularly near RM 620 at Lakeway Blvd), State Highway 71 (particularly near SH-71 at RM 620), Lakeway Boulevard (particularly near Lakeway Blvd at RM 620). These roads see heavy traffic and multiple factors contribute to their high accident rates.

Which hospitals treat 18-wheeler accident injuries in Lakeway?

Due to the severity of 18-wheeler crash injuries, victims typically need trauma center care. Baylor Scott & White Medical Center - Lakeway provides Level I or Level II trauma services for the serious injuries these accidents cause, including traumatic brain injuries and spinal cord damage.

How long does an 18-wheeler accident lawsuit take?

These complex cases typically take 12-24 months when litigation is required. Some cases settle faster with strong evidence and cooperative defendants. We work to resolve cases efficiently while ensuring full compensation for our clients.

What is hours-of-service and why does it matter?

Hours-of-service rules limit how long truckers can drive without rest (11 hours driving in a 14-hour window after 10 hours off). Violations cause driver fatigue, a leading cause of 18-wheeler accidents. ELD data can prove these violations.
