What to Do After a Car Accident in Texas: A Step-by-Step Guide

Car Accidents • By Israel Medina, Esq. • January 2025

The moments after a car accident are chaotic. Your heart is racing, adrenaline is pumping, and you might be in pain. But what you do in the next few hours and days can make a massive difference in your ability to recover compensation later.

Here's exactly what to do after a car accident in Texas—step by step.

1 Check for Injuries and Get to Safety

Your health comes first. Before worrying about your car, the other driver, or anything else:

  • Check yourself for injuries—some injuries don't hurt immediately due to adrenaline
  • Check on your passengers
  • If possible and safe, move your vehicle out of traffic to avoid additional collisions
  • Turn on your hazard lights

If anyone is seriously injured, call 911 immediately. Don't try to move someone with a potential neck or spine injury unless there's an immediate danger like fire.

2 Call the Police

In Texas, you're required to report any accident that results in injury, death, or property damage over $1,000. But even for minor fender-benders, having a police report is valuable for your insurance claim.

When officers arrive:

  • Stick to the facts—describe what happened without admitting fault
  • Mention any injuries, even if they seem minor
  • Get the responding officer's name and badge number
  • Ask how to obtain a copy of the accident report

Important: Never say "I'm sorry" or "It was my fault" at the scene—even if you think it might have been. These statements can be used against you later. Just state the facts of what happened.

3 Document Everything

Your smartphone is your best friend here. Take photos and videos of:

  • All vehicles involved (damage, license plates, positions on the road)
  • The accident scene (traffic signs, road conditions, skid marks)
  • Your injuries (cuts, bruises, swelling)
  • The other driver's insurance card and driver's license
  • Any visible damage to property (guardrails, signs, etc.)

Also collect:

  • Names and contact info for all drivers and passengers
  • Insurance information from all parties
  • Names and contact info for any witnesses

4 Seek Medical Attention—Even If You Feel Fine

This is where many people make a costly mistake. After an accident, adrenaline masks pain. Some injuries—like whiplash, concussions, and internal bleeding—don't show symptoms for hours or even days.

Go to an emergency room or urgent care clinic within 24-72 hours of the accident. This creates a medical record linking your injuries to the crash, which is essential for your claim.

If you wait weeks to see a doctor, the insurance company will argue that your injuries either weren't caused by the accident or aren't as serious as you claim. Don't give them that ammunition.

5 Report the Accident to Your Insurance Company

Texas requires you to notify your insurance company about an accident. Do this promptly—usually within a few days—but keep your statement brief and factual.

What to say:

  • When and where the accident occurred
  • Basic facts about what happened
  • That you're seeking medical treatment

What NOT to say:

  • Don't admit fault
  • Don't speculate about what happened
  • Don't give a recorded statement to the other driver's insurance without talking to a lawyer first

6 Don't Accept a Quick Settlement

Insurance adjusters are trained to settle claims fast and cheap. They may call you within days offering a settlement that sounds reasonable. But here's the problem: you don't yet know the full extent of your injuries or damages.

Once you accept a settlement, you can't go back and ask for more—even if you need surgery six months later. That $5,000 "quick settlement" could cost you $50,000 or more in the long run.

7 Talk to a Personal Injury Lawyer

For minor fender-benders with no injuries, you might not need an attorney. But if you have any injuries, talking to a lawyer is almost always worth it.

Consider talking to a lawyer if:

  • You were injured (even soft tissue injuries like whiplash)
  • The other driver was uninsured or underinsured
  • Fault is disputed
  • The insurance company is giving you the runaround
  • A commercial vehicle (truck, delivery van, rideshare) was involved

Most personal injury lawyers offer free consultations and work on contingency—meaning you pay nothing unless you win.

The Bottom Line

The actions you take after a car accident can protect your health, your rights, and your financial recovery. Stay calm, document everything, get medical attention, and don't rush into a settlement.
