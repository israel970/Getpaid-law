Why You Need a Box Truck Accident Lawyer

Box trucks are everywhere - delivering furniture, moving households, and transporting cargo. Many are driven by inexperienced operators who underestimate the size and handling characteristics of these vehicles.

Unlike semi-trucks, box trucks can often be driven without a commercial license, leading to many unqualified drivers on the road. Our attorneys understand the unique issues in box truck accident cases.

Types of Box Truck Accident Cases

Moving Truck Accidents

U-Haul, Penske, and Budget rental trucks driven by inexperienced operators.

Height Clearance Crashes

Box trucks striking bridges and overpasses due to driver error.

Wide Turn Accidents

Drivers unfamiliar with the trucks size making turns that strike other vehicles.

Wind-Related Accidents

High-profile box trucks blown over or pushed into other lanes by wind.

Rear-End Collisions

Box trucks with poor braking performance crashing into stopped traffic.

Pedestrian Accidents

Box trucks striking pedestrians in parking lots and residential areas.

Proving Liability in Box Truck Accident Cases

Establishing liability in truck accident cases requires thorough investigation and understanding of federal trucking regulations. Key elements we examine include:

  • Driver Negligence: Speeding, distracted driving, fatigue, or impairment
  • Company Negligence: Inadequate training, pressure to violate hours of service, poor maintenance
  • Regulatory Violations: FMCSA violations, overweight loads, improper cargo securement
  • Equipment Failures: Defective brakes, tires, or other mechanical components

Frequently Asked Questions

Do box truck drivers need special licenses?

Many box trucks under 26,000 pounds can be driven with a regular license, meaning drivers may lack commercial vehicle training.

Who is liable for a rental truck accident?

Potentially liable parties include the driver, the rental company (for negligent maintenance), and any company that hired the driver.

What if the box truck driver was inexperienced?

If the rental company or employer failed to verify the drivers qualifications, this may establish additional liability.

How are box truck accidents different from car accidents?

Box trucks cause more severe injuries due to their size and weight. Cases may also involve commercial insurance policies with higher limits.
