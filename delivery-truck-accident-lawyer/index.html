Why You Need a Delivery Truck Accident Lawyer

The explosion of e-commerce has put more delivery trucks on the road than ever before. Drivers for Amazon, FedEx, UPS, and countless other companies face intense pressure to meet delivery quotas, often leading to speeding, distracted driving, and fatigue-related accidents.

These companies use complex contractor relationships to try to avoid liability. Our attorneys know how to pierce these corporate shields and hold the right parties accountable for your injuries.

Proving Liability in Delivery Truck Accident Cases

Establishing liability in truck accident cases requires thorough investigation and understanding of federal trucking regulations. Key elements we examine include:

  • Driver Negligence: Speeding, distracted driving, fatigue, or impairment
  • Company Negligence: Inadequate training, pressure to violate hours of service, poor maintenance
  • Regulatory Violations: FMCSA violations, overweight loads, improper cargo securement
  • Equipment Failures: Defective brakes, tires, or other mechanical components

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I sue Amazon for a delivery driver accident?

Yes, even though many Amazon drivers are contractors, Amazon can still be held liable through various legal theories including negligent hiring and agency relationships.

What if the delivery driver was a contractor?

Companies cannot escape liability simply by using contractors. We investigate the level of control the company exercised over the driver.

How do delivery truck accidents differ from regular car accidents?

These cases often involve corporate defendants with more resources, require understanding of employment relationships, and may have higher insurance coverage.

What compensation can I receive?

You may recover medical expenses, lost wages, pain and suffering, and in some cases punitive damages against companies with negligent policies.
