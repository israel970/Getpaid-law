Common Motorcycle Accident Scenarios

Left-Turn Accidents

The most common motorcycle crash—when cars turn left in front of oncoming motorcycles they failed to see or misjudged the speed of.

Lane Change Crashes

Drivers fail to check blind spots before changing lanes, colliding with motorcycles riding alongside them.

Rear-End Collisions

Distracted drivers fail to see stopped or slowing motorcycles and rear-end them at full speed.

Door Opening Accidents

Parked vehicle occupants open doors into the path of approaching motorcycles, causing crashes.

Road Hazard Crashes

Potholes, debris, gravel, and oil slicks cause motorcycle crashes that may create liability for road maintainers.

Intersection Collisions

Drivers running red lights or stop signs strike motorcycles with right of way.

Texas Motorcycle Accident Laws

Understanding Texas law is crucial for maximizing your recovery. Here are key legal principles affecting motorcycle accident cases:

  • Helmet Law: Texas requires helmets for riders under 21 and those without specific insurance or safety course completion. However, not wearing a helmet doesn't bar recovery or prove fault.
  • Lane Splitting: Texas does not currently allow lane splitting (riding between lanes of traffic). However, bills to allow it have been introduced.
  • Comparative Fault: Texas applies modified comparative fault to motorcycle cases. Riders can recover if less than 51% at fault, with damages reduced by their fault percentage.
  • Motorcycle Equipment: Texas law requires specific equipment including mirrors, turn signals, and lighting. Equipment violations don't automatically establish rider fault in crashes.

Frequently Asked Questions About Dallas Motorcycle Accidents

Will I be blamed for my motorcycle accident just because I was riding?

Unfortunately, bias against motorcyclists exists. Our attorneys counter this by presenting clear evidence of the other driver's negligence, using expert witnesses, and educating jurors about motorcycle safety and right-of-way laws.

What compensation can I receive for a motorcycle accident?

Motorcycle accident victims can recover medical expenses, lost wages, pain and suffering, property damage, and sometimes punitive damages. Because injuries tend to be severe, settlements are often significant.

Does not wearing a helmet affect my case in Texas?

Texas doesn't require helmets for all riders, and not wearing one doesn't make you automatically at fault. However, the defense may argue your head injuries would have been less severe with a helmet, potentially reducing damages.

What are the most dangerous roads for motorcycles in Dallas?

In Dallas, motorcycle accident accidents frequently occur on Interstate 35E (particularly near I-35E at I-30 interchange), Interstate 30 (R.L. Thornton Freeway) (particularly near I-30 at I-35E), Interstate 635 (LBJ Freeway) (particularly near I-635 at US-75). These roads see heavy traffic and multiple factors contribute to their high accident rates.

Where should I go for medical treatment after a motorcycle crash in Dallas?

For serious injuries, get to the nearest trauma center immediately. Parkland Memorial Hospital provides trauma care for the severe injuries common in motorcycle crashes. For less serious injuries, urgent care can document everything for your case.

How long do motorcycle accident cases take to settle?

Motorcycle cases often take longer than car accident cases because injuries are more severe and require longer treatment. Most cases settle within 6-18 months, though complex cases requiring litigation take longer.

Can I sue if road conditions caused my motorcycle crash?

Yes, in some cases. If potholes, debris, or inadequate maintenance contributed to your crash, the government entity responsible for the road may be liable. These cases have special requirements and shorter deadlines.

What should I tell the insurance company after a motorcycle accident?

As little as possible. Insurance adjusters look for ways to blame motorcyclists. Don't give recorded statements, don't speculate about fault, and don't sign anything without consulting an attorney first.
