Types of Brain Injuries

Concussions

Often called 'mild TBI,' concussions can cause persistent headaches, memory problems, and cognitive difficulties lasting months or years.

Contusions

Brain bruises from direct impact can cause localized damage affecting specific functions depending on location.

Diffuse Axonal Injury

Rotation or shaking damages nerve connections throughout the brain, often from car accidents.

Penetrating Injuries

Objects penetrating the skull cause direct brain damage and high infection risk.

Coup-Contrecoup

Impact causes injury at both the impact site and the opposite side of the brain as it bounces within the skull.

Second Impact Syndrome

Suffering a second concussion before fully recovering from the first can cause severe brain swelling.

Texas Brain Injury Laws

Understanding Texas law is crucial for maximizing your recovery. Here are key legal principles affecting brain injury cases:

  • Future Damages: Texas allows recovery of future medical expenses, lost earning capacity, and pain and suffering when brain injuries cause permanent impairment.
  • Life Care Plans: Courts accept expert life care plans projecting future medical needs, which can extend for decades and cost millions of dollars.
  • Loss of Consortium: Spouses can recover for loss of companionship and support when brain injuries change their partner's personality or abilities.
  • No Damage Caps: Texas doesn't cap economic or non-economic damages in most personal injury cases, allowing full recovery for brain injury victims.

Frequently Asked Questions About Rio Grande Valley Brain Injurys

What are symptoms of traumatic brain injury?

TBI symptoms include headaches, dizziness, nausea, memory problems, difficulty concentrating, mood changes, sleep disturbances, sensitivity to light and sound, and personality changes. Symptoms may appear immediately or develop over days or weeks.

How much compensation can I receive for a brain injury?

Brain injury settlements vary widely based on severity. Mild TBIs may settle for tens of thousands, while severe TBIs requiring lifetime care often result in multi-million dollar awards covering future medical care, lost earnings, and quality of life impacts.

What medical evidence is needed for a brain injury case?

Medical records, imaging studies (CT, MRI), neuropsychological testing, and expert medical opinions document brain injuries. We work with neurologists, neuropsychologists, and other specialists to fully document your injury.

What is a life care plan?

A life care plan is a detailed projection of all future medical care, therapy, assistance, and equipment a brain injury victim will need. Prepared by certified life care planners, these documents can show lifetime care costs in the millions of dollars.

Where can I get brain injury treatment in Rio Grande Valley?

For acute brain injuries, trauma centers provide emergency care—Valley Baptist Medical Center - Harlingen has neurology and neurosurgery capabilities. Long-term rehabilitation is available at specialized facilities. We can recommend treatment providers experienced with TBI.

How long does it take to recover from a brain injury?

Recovery varies dramatically. Some concussion symptoms resolve in weeks, while severe TBIs may cause permanent impairment. Maximum medical improvement may take 2-3 years, but some recovery continues beyond that. Your legal case should account for long-term prognosis.

Can I sue for a concussion?

Yes. While often called 'mild' TBI, concussions can cause significant symptoms and impact quality of life. If someone's negligence caused your concussion, you deserve compensation for medical expenses, lost wages, pain, and any lasting effects.

What causes most brain injuries in Rio Grande Valley?

In Rio Grande Valley, brain injuries commonly result from motor vehicle accidents on roads like US Expressway 83 (I-2), US Highway 281, US Highway 77 and Interstate 69E/US-77, workplace incidents, falls, and assaults. Car and truck accidents cause particularly severe TBIs due to the forces involved.
