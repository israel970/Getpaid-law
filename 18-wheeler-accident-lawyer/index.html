Why You Need a 18-Wheeler Accident Lawyer

18-wheelers, also known as semi-trucks or tractor-trailers, can weigh up to 80,000 pounds fully loaded. When these massive vehicles collide with passenger cars, the results are often catastrophic. Victims frequently suffer life-altering injuries including spinal cord damage, traumatic brain injuries, and multiple fractures.

These cases involve complex federal regulations, multiple liable parties, and aggressive insurance companies with teams of lawyers. You need an experienced 18-wheeler accident attorney who understands trucking industry practices and can fight for the full compensation you deserve.

Types of 18-Wheeler Accident Cases

Jackknife Accidents

When the trailer swings out to form a 90-degree angle with the cab, often striking multiple vehicles.

Underride Crashes

When a smaller vehicle slides under the trailer, often causing fatal roof crush injuries.

Tire Blowouts

Catastrophic tire failures that cause drivers to lose control of 80,000-pound vehicles.

Brake Failures

When overloaded trucks or poorly maintained brakes cannot stop in time.

Wide Turn Accidents

When trucks swing wide and crush vehicles in adjacent lanes.

Blind Spot Collisions

Large trucks have massive blind spots that can hide entire vehicles.

Proving Liability in 18-Wheeler Accident Cases

Establishing liability in truck accident cases requires thorough investigation and understanding of federal trucking regulations. Key elements we examine include:

  • Driver Negligence: Speeding, distracted driving, fatigue, or impairment
  • Company Negligence: Inadequate training, pressure to violate hours of service, poor maintenance
  • Regulatory Violations: FMCSA violations, overweight loads, improper cargo securement
  • Equipment Failures: Defective brakes, tires, or other mechanical components

Compensation for 18-Wheeler Accident Victims

Truck accident victims may be entitled to significant compensation including:

Medical Expenses

Emergency care, surgeries, rehabilitation, and ongoing treatment costs

Lost Income

Current and future lost wages, reduced earning capacity

Pain & Suffering

Physical pain, emotional distress, loss of enjoyment of life

Punitive Damages

Additional damages when trucking companies act with gross negligence

Frequently Asked Questions

Who can be held liable in an 18-wheeler accident?

Multiple parties may be liable including the truck driver, trucking company, cargo loader, truck manufacturer, and maintenance companies. An experienced attorney will investigate all potential defendants.

What federal regulations apply to 18-wheelers?

The FMCSA regulates hours of service, maintenance requirements, driver qualifications, and cargo loading. Violations of these regulations can establish negligence.

How much is my 18-wheeler accident case worth?

These cases often result in significant settlements due to severe injuries and multiple liable parties. Compensation depends on your injuries, lost wages, and long-term care needs.

What evidence is important in truck accident cases?

Critical evidence includes the trucks electronic logging device (ELD) data, maintenance records, driver logs, black box data, and cargo manifests.
