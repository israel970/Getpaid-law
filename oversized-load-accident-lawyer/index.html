Why You Need a Oversized Load Accident Lawyer

Oversized loads - including construction equipment, wind turbine blades, prefabricated buildings, and industrial machinery - require special permits and safety measures. When trucking companies cut corners, devastating accidents occur.

These cases involve complex regulations about permits, escort vehicles, routing, and time-of-day restrictions. Our attorneys have experience holding negligent carriers accountable for oversized load accidents.

Types of Oversized Load Accident Cases

Wide Load Collisions

Oversized loads striking vehicles in adjacent lanes or oncoming traffic.

Fallen Load Accidents

Improperly secured oversized cargo falling onto the roadway.

Escort Vehicle Failures

Accidents caused by missing or negligent pilot car escorts.

Bridge Strikes

Oversized loads striking bridges and overpasses due to improper routing.

Utility Line Accidents

Tall loads striking power lines and causing electrocution hazards.

Permit Violation Crashes

Accidents caused by carriers ignoring permit restrictions on routes and timing.

Proving Liability in Oversized Load Accident Cases

Establishing liability in truck accident cases requires thorough investigation and understanding of federal trucking regulations. Key elements we examine include:

  • Driver Negligence: Speeding, distracted driving, fatigue, or impairment
  • Company Negligence: Inadequate training, pressure to violate hours of service, poor maintenance
  • Regulatory Violations: FMCSA violations, overweight loads, improper cargo securement
  • Equipment Failures: Defective brakes, tires, or other mechanical components

Frequently Asked Questions

What regulations apply to oversized loads?

Oversized loads require special permits specifying routes, times of travel, required escort vehicles, and maximum speeds.

Who is liable for an oversized load accident?

Liable parties may include the trucking company, load owner, permit applicant, and escort vehicle operators.

What if the load fell off the truck?

Improperly secured loads indicate negligent loading practices. Both the carrier and the party that loaded the cargo may be liable.

How do escort vehicle failures cause accidents?

Pilot cars warn other motorists and help navigate obstacles. When they fail to do their job, other vehicles may not have time to avoid the oversized load.
