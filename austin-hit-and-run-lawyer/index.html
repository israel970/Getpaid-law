Texas Hit and Run Laws

Understanding Texas law is crucial for maximizing your recovery. Here are key legal principles affecting hit and run cases:

  • Hit and Run Penalties: Failing to stop after an accident is a Class C misdemeanor if only property damage occurs, but becomes a felony if someone is injured (up to 5 years) or killed (up to 10 years).
  • Duty to Stop: Texas law requires all drivers involved in accidents to immediately stop, render aid if needed, and exchange information including name, address, and insurance.
  • UM/UIM Coverage: Texas insurers must offer uninsured/underinsured motorist coverage, which provides compensation when hit and run drivers can't be found or lack adequate insurance.
  • Statute of Limitations: You have two years to file a lawsuit, but investigation to identify hit and run drivers should begin immediately.

Frequently Asked Questions About Austin Hit and Runs

What should I do right after a hit and run?

Call 911 immediately. Note any details you remember about the vehicle—make, model, color, license plate, direction of travel. Look for witnesses. Check nearby businesses for surveillance cameras. Document your injuries and damage. Contact an attorney before calling insurance.

Can I get compensation if the hit and run driver is never found?

Yes. Your own uninsured motorist (UM) coverage provides compensation when at-fault drivers can't be identified. We also investigate whether other parties may share liability, such as employers of commercial drivers.

How do attorneys find hit and run drivers?

We canvas the area for surveillance footage, interview witnesses, work with police investigators, hire private investigators, search social media, and review local body shop records for vehicles matching your description.

What if I only got a partial license plate?

Partial plate information combined with vehicle description can narrow down possibilities. Police and private investigators can search DMV records for matching vehicles in the area.

Where do most hit and run accidents happen in Austin?

Hit and run accidents in Austin commonly occur on busy roads like Interstate 35 (I-35), MoPac Expressway (Loop 1), US Highway 183 and US Highway 290 East where drivers believe they can escape unnoticed. Parking lot hit and runs are also common.

Which hospitals treat hit and run accident victims in Austin?

Seek immediate medical attention regardless of severity—hit and run victims often have adrenaline masking serious injuries. For serious injuries, Dell Seton Medical Center at The University of Texas provides trauma care in the Austin area.

How long do hit and run cases take?

If the driver is quickly identified, cases proceed like typical accident claims (6-18 months). Uninsured motorist claims against your own insurance may resolve faster. Cases where extensive investigation is needed take longer.

Does my insurance go up if I file a hit and run claim?

Filing a UM/UIM claim for a hit and run generally shouldn't increase your rates since you weren't at fault. However, insurance practices vary. This is another reason to consult an attorney before filing.
