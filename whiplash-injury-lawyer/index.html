Understanding Your Whiplash Injury Case

Whiplash is one of the most common—and most misunderstood—injuries from car accidents. Insurance companies love to dismiss whiplash as a minor injury, but the reality is that whiplash can cause chronic pain, headaches, and disability lasting months or even years. Our network of experienced whiplash attorneys knows how to prove these injuries and fight for the compensation you deserve.

Types of Whiplash Injury Cases We Handle

Our network of attorneys has experience with all types of whiplash injury cases.

Grade 1 Whiplash

Neck pain and stiffness without physical signs of injury.

Grade 2 Whiplash

Neck pain with decreased range of motion and point tenderness.

Grade 3 Whiplash

Neck pain with neurological signs like weakness or sensory deficits.

Grade 4 Whiplash

Neck pain with fracture or dislocation.

Whiplash Associated Disorder

Long-term symptoms including cognitive problems and psychological effects.

Chronic Whiplash

Symptoms persisting more than 6 months after injury.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does whiplash last?
Most whiplash injuries heal within 3-6 months, but 12-50% of people develop chronic symptoms. Factors affecting recovery include severity of impact, age, prior neck problems, and how quickly treatment begins.
Can you get a settlement for whiplash?
Yes. Whiplash settlements range from $2,500 for minor cases to over $100,000 for severe or chronic whiplash with long-term effects. Cases with documented treatment and lasting symptoms have higher values.
How do you prove whiplash?
Whiplash is proven through medical records, MRI or X-ray imaging, physical examination findings, documented treatment history, and testimony about how symptoms affect your daily life.
Is whiplash serious?
It can be. While many people recover fully, others develop chronic pain, headaches, cognitive problems, and even depression. Never dismiss whiplash without proper medical evaluation and treatment.
