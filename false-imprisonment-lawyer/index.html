Understanding False Imprisonment Claims

False imprisonment occurs when you are confined or restrained against your will without legal justification. This can happen during police encounters, in jails, or even in private settings.

Whether you were held too long after arrest, detained without charges, or wrongfully held by security guards, you have legal recourse. Our attorneys fight for victims of unlawful confinement.

Types of False Imprisonment Cases

Extended Detention

Being held longer than legally permitted after arrest without charges.

Detention Without Charges

Confinement without formal charges or arraignment.

Immigration Detention

Unlawful detention by immigration authorities.

Security Guard Detention

Private security illegally detaining shoppers or visitors.

Psychiatric Holds

Improper involuntary psychiatric commitment.

Bail Violations

Being held despite posting bail or meeting release conditions.

Filing a False Imprisonment Lawsuit

Civil rights claims against police are brought under 42 U.S.C. ยง 1983, which allows victims to sue government officials who violate constitutional rights. Key elements include:

  • Constitutional Violation: Proving the officer violated a specific constitutional right
  • Acting Under Color of Law: The officer was acting in their official capacity
  • Qualified Immunity: Overcoming the officers defense that their conduct was not clearly unconstitutional
  • Municipal Liability: Potentially holding the department liable for patterns of misconduct

Frequently Asked Questions

How long can police hold me without charges?

Time limits vary by jurisdiction, but generally police must charge you within 48-72 hours or release you.

What is the difference between false arrest and false imprisonment?

False arrest involves unlawful seizure while false imprisonment focuses on unlawful confinement. Both can occur together or separately.

Can private citizens commit false imprisonment?

Yes, security guards, store employees, and others can be liable for unlawfully detaining individuals.

What compensation is available?

Victims can recover damages for lost wages, emotional distress, humiliation, and in some cases punitive damages.
