Types of Austin Car Accidents We Handle

Our network of car accident attorneys handles all types of vehicle collisions.

Rear-End Collisions

The most common type of accident in Austin traffic. Even "minor" rear-end crashes can cause whiplash, herniated discs, and long-term neck and back problems.

T-Bone Accidents

Side-impact crashes at intersections can cause catastrophic injuries. Drivers who run red lights or fail to yield are typically liable.

Head-On Collisions

The most dangerous type of crash. Often caused by wrong-way drivers, distracted driving, or drivers crossing the center line.

Hit and Run Accidents

If a driver fled the scene, you may still have options. Your UM/UIM coverage can provide compensation while police search for the driver.

Drunk Driving Accidents

Drunk drivers can be held liable for both compensatory and punitive damages. Austin's bar scene means DUI accidents are unfortunately common.

Distracted Driving

Texting, eating, GPS use—distracted driving is now the leading cause of accidents. Phone records can prove the other driver was at fault.

Uber & Lyft Accidents

Rideshare accidents involve complex insurance issues. The driver's status (app on, passenger in car) determines which insurance applies.

Uninsured Drivers

Texas has one of the highest uninsured driver rates in the country. Your own UM/UIM coverage is critical for protection.

Multi-Vehicle Pileups

Common on I-35 and Highway 130. Multiple liable parties and insurance companies make these cases complex—but often more valuable.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does an Austin car accident lawyer cost?
Most Austin car accident lawyers work on contingency, meaning you pay nothing upfront. The attorney only gets paid if you win your case, typically 33% of your settlement. You won't pay any out-of-pocket costs for legal representation.
How long do I have to file a car accident claim in Texas?
In Texas, you have 2 years from the date of the car accident to file a personal injury lawsuit. However, if your claim involves a government vehicle or entity, you may have as little as 6 months to file a notice of claim. Contact an attorney as soon as possible to protect your rights.
What should I do immediately after a car accident in Austin?
After an Austin car accident: (1) Call 911 and stay at the scene, (2) Seek medical attention even if you feel fine, (3) Take photos of all vehicles, injuries, and the scene, (4) Exchange information with other drivers but don't admit fault, (5) Get contact info from witnesses, (6) Report the accident to your insurance, (7) Contact a car accident lawyer before giving recorded statements.
How much is my Austin car accident case worth?
Car accident case values depend on medical expenses, lost wages, pain and suffering, and the severity of injuries. Minor accidents might settle for $10,000-$25,000, while serious injuries involving surgery or permanent disability can be worth $100,000 to several million dollars.
What if the other driver doesn't have insurance?
Texas has one of the highest uninsured driver rates in the country. If you're hit by an uninsured driver, you can file a claim with your own uninsured/underinsured motorist (UM/UIM) coverage. An attorney can help you maximize recovery from all available sources.
Can I still recover damages if I was partially at fault?
Yes. Texas follows a 'modified comparative fault' rule. You can recover damages as long as you're not more than 50% at fault. However, your compensation is reduced by your percentage of fault.
