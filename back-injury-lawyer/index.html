Understanding Your Back Injury Case

Back injuries are among the most common—and most debilitating—results of accidents. From herniated discs to fractured vertebrae, back injuries can cause chronic pain that affects every aspect of your life. Insurance companies often dismiss back pain as pre-existing or exaggerated. Our network of back injury attorneys knows how to document these injuries and fight for the compensation you deserve.

Types of Back Injury Cases We Handle

Our network of attorneys has experience with all types of back injury cases.

Herniated Disc

Disc material pushes out and presses on spinal nerves, causing pain and numbness.

Bulging Disc

Disc extends beyond its normal boundary, potentially compressing nerves.

Fractured Vertebrae

Broken bones in the spine from trauma or compression.

Lumbar Strain

Damage to muscles and ligaments in the lower back.

Sciatica

Pain radiating down the leg from compressed sciatic nerve.

Degenerative Disc Disease

Accident can accelerate existing disc degeneration.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much is a back injury case worth?
Back injury settlements range from $10,000 for minor strains to over $1 million for injuries requiring surgery or causing permanent disability. The value depends on medical treatment needed, impact on your work, and long-term prognosis.
Can I get compensation if I had a pre-existing back condition?
Yes. Under the 'eggshell plaintiff' doctrine, defendants take victims as they find them. If an accident aggravated a pre-existing condition, you can recover for the worsening of your condition.
What if my back pain didn't start until days after the accident?
Delayed onset back pain is common after accidents. Adrenaline and inflammation can mask symptoms initially. Document when symptoms began and see a doctor promptly. This delay doesn't prevent you from filing a claim.
Do I need surgery for my back injury claim?
No. Many back injury cases settle without surgery. However, cases involving surgery or recommendations for future surgery typically have higher values due to increased medical costs and severity.
