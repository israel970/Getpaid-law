Why You Need a Oil Field Injury Lawyer

The oil and gas industry is one of the most dangerous in America. Workers face risks from explosions, toxic chemicals, heavy equipment, and extreme conditions. When companies cut corners on safety to increase production, workers pay the price.

Oil field injuries often involve multiple responsible parties: drilling companies, equipment manufacturers, staffing agencies, and service contractors. Our attorneys know how to identify all liable parties and maximize your recovery.

Types of Oil Field Injury Cases

Rig Explosions

Blowouts and explosions from uncontrolled well pressure and equipment failures.

Equipment Failures

Injuries from defective drilling equipment, pipes, and machinery.

Chemical Exposure

Exposure to hydrogen sulfide, benzene, and other toxic oilfield chemicals.

Transportation Accidents

Crashes involving oilfield trucks, tankers, and service vehicles.

Slip and Fall

Falls on slippery rig surfaces and from elevated drilling platforms.

Struck-By Injuries

Workers struck by pipes, drilling equipment, and falling objects.

Proving Liability in Oil Field Injury Cases

Establishing liability in workplace injury cases requires thorough investigation and understanding of OSHA regulations and industry standards. Key elements we examine include:

  • Employer Negligence: Failure to provide safe working conditions, proper training, or required safety equipment
  • OSHA Violations: Documented safety violations that contributed to your injury
  • Equipment Defects: Faulty machinery or tools that caused or contributed to the accident
  • Third-Party Liability: Contractors, property owners, or manufacturers who share responsibility

Compensation for Oil Field Injury Victims

Workplace injury victims may be entitled to compensation beyond workers compensation including:

Medical Expenses

Emergency care, surgeries, rehabilitation, and ongoing treatment costs

Lost Income

Current and future lost wages, reduced earning capacity

Pain & Suffering

Physical pain, emotional distress, loss of enjoyment of life

Permanent Disability

Compensation for lasting impairments affecting your ability to work and live

Frequently Asked Questions

What makes oil field injury cases different?

Oil field cases often involve multiple defendants, complex equipment, industry-specific regulations, and higher damage awards due to severe injuries.

Who is liable for an oil field accident?

Drilling companies, equipment manufacturers, service contractors, staffing agencies, and landowners may all share liability.

What regulations apply to oil field safety?

OSHA regulations apply, along with state oil and gas commission rules and industry standards.

How much are oil field injury cases worth?

Due to the severity of injuries and multiple liable parties, oil field cases often result in significant settlements and verdicts.
