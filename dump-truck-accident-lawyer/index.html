Why You Need a Dump Truck Accident Lawyer

Dump trucks are essential to construction and mining operations, but they are also among the most dangerous vehicles on the road. When fully loaded with gravel, sand, or debris, these trucks can weigh 60,000 pounds or more and have significant blind spots.

Many dump truck accidents involve construction companies cutting corners on safety and maintenance. Our attorneys investigate these practices and hold negligent companies accountable.

Types of Dump Truck Accident Cases

Raised Bed Accidents

Dump trucks striking overpasses and power lines with raised beds.

Falling Load Accidents

Unsecured loads falling onto following vehicles causing crashes and injuries.

Runaway Truck Crashes

Overloaded dump trucks losing brakes on steep grades.

Construction Zone Accidents

Dump trucks striking workers and equipment at job sites.

Blind Spot Collisions

Drivers unable to see vehicles and pedestrians beside and behind the truck.

Tip-Over Accidents

Dump trucks rolling over while dumping or on uneven terrain.

Proving Liability in Dump Truck Accident Cases

Establishing liability in truck accident cases requires thorough investigation and understanding of federal trucking regulations. Key elements we examine include:

  • Driver Negligence: Speeding, distracted driving, fatigue, or impairment
  • Company Negligence: Inadequate training, pressure to violate hours of service, poor maintenance
  • Regulatory Violations: FMCSA violations, overweight loads, improper cargo securement
  • Equipment Failures: Defective brakes, tires, or other mechanical components

Frequently Asked Questions

Who is liable for a dump truck accident?

Liable parties may include the truck driver, trucking company, construction contractor, cargo loader, and truck owner.

What if the dump truck was overloaded?

Overloading violates federal regulations and significantly increases accident risk. This can establish negligence against multiple parties.

How do dump truck blind spots cause accidents?

Dump trucks have large blind spots on all sides. Drivers may not see motorcycles, bicycles, or even cars before changing lanes or turning.

What evidence is important in dump truck cases?

Key evidence includes load tickets, weight station records, maintenance logs, and any surveillance footage from the construction site.
