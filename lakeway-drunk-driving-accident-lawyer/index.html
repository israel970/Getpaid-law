Texas Drunk Driving Accident Laws

Understanding Texas law is crucial for maximizing your recovery. Here are key legal principles affecting drunk driving accident cases:

  • Per Se Intoxication: Texas considers drivers legally intoxicated at .08 BAC or higher. Higher BAC levels (.15+) result in enhanced criminal penalties and stronger civil cases.
  • Dram Shop Act: Texas allows claims against alcohol providers who serve obviously intoxicated adults or any alcohol to minors who then cause accidents.
  • Punitive Damages: Drunk driving typically meets the 'gross negligence' standard for punitive damages, which are capped at the greater of $200,000 or twice economic damages plus equal non-economic damages.
  • Criminal vs. Civil: Criminal conviction isn't required for civil recovery. Different standards of proof apply—civil cases require preponderance of evidence, not beyond reasonable doubt.

Frequently Asked Questions About Lakeway Drunk Driving Accidents

Can I get more compensation because the driver was drunk?

Yes. Drunk driving typically qualifies for punitive damages in addition to compensatory damages. Courts award punitive damages to punish reckless behavior and deter others. These can substantially increase your recovery.

What is a dram shop claim?

Texas's Dram Shop Act allows claims against bars, restaurants, and stores that serve alcohol to obviously intoxicated persons or minors who then cause accidents. This adds another source of recovery beyond the drunk driver's insurance.

How does the criminal case affect my civil claim?

The cases are separate but related. Criminal conviction isn't required for civil recovery, but conviction can help establish liability. Police reports, BAC test results, and other criminal evidence can be used in your civil case.

What if the drunk driver doesn't have insurance?

You may have claims against bars that over-served them (dram shop), your own uninsured motorist coverage, or other liable parties. Our attorneys investigate all potential sources of recovery.

Where do most drunk driving accidents occur in Lakeway?

Drunk driving accidents in Lakeway are concentrated near entertainment districts, bars, and restaurants, particularly on weekend nights. However, impaired driving occurs throughout the area on highways like RM 620, State Highway 71, Lakeway Boulevard and Lohmans Crossing Road.

Which hospitals treat drunk driving accident victims in Lakeway?

Drunk driving accidents often cause severe injuries requiring trauma care. Baylor Scott & White Medical Center - Lakeway provides emergency and trauma services for serious accident injuries in the Lakeway area.

How long do drunk driving accident cases take?

These cases often take 12-24 months because they may involve waiting for criminal case resolution, investigating dram shop claims, and building strong punitive damages arguments. Complex cases with multiple defendants take longer.

Can I sue the bar that served the drunk driver?

Potentially yes, under Texas's Dram Shop Act. If the bar served someone who was obviously intoxicated, or served any alcohol to a minor, and that person then caused your accident, the bar may share liability.
