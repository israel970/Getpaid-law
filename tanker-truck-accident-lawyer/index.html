Why You Need a Tanker Truck Accident Lawyer

Tanker trucks carry gasoline, diesel, chemicals, and other hazardous materials across our highways. When these trucks crash, the consequences extend far beyond typical traffic accidents - explosions, fires, and toxic spills can affect entire communities.

These cases involve strict federal hazmat regulations, environmental contamination issues, and potentially hundreds of affected victims. Our attorneys have the resources to handle these complex, high-stakes cases.

Types of Tanker Truck Accident Cases

Fuel Tanker Explosions

Gasoline and diesel tanker crashes that result in massive explosions and fires.

Chemical Spill Accidents

Hazardous chemical releases that can cause burns, respiratory damage, and long-term health effects.

Liquid Surge Rollovers

Partially filled tankers that roll over when liquid cargo shifts during turns.

Loading Dock Accidents

Explosions and spills during fuel loading and unloading operations.

Highway Fires

Tanker fires that close highways and endanger other motorists.

Environmental Contamination

Spills that contaminate soil, groundwater, and affect nearby residents.

Proving Liability in Tanker Truck Accident Cases

Establishing liability in truck accident cases requires thorough investigation and understanding of federal trucking regulations. Key elements we examine include:

  • Driver Negligence: Speeding, distracted driving, fatigue, or impairment
  • Company Negligence: Inadequate training, pressure to violate hours of service, poor maintenance
  • Regulatory Violations: FMCSA violations, overweight loads, improper cargo securement
  • Equipment Failures: Defective brakes, tires, or other mechanical components

Frequently Asked Questions

What makes tanker accidents more dangerous than other truck accidents?

Tanker cargo can explode, burn, or release toxic chemicals. Liquid cargo also shifts during movement, making these trucks harder to control.

What regulations apply to tanker trucks?

Tankers carrying hazardous materials must comply with DOT hazmat regulations, including special licensing, placarding, and routing requirements.

Can I sue if I was affected by a chemical spill?

Yes, if you suffered health effects from a tanker spill, you may have a claim even if your vehicle was not directly involved in the crash.

What compensation is available for tanker accident victims?

Victims may recover damages for burns, respiratory injuries, cancer risks, property damage, and business losses from evacuations.
