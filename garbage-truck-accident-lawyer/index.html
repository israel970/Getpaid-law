Why You Need a Garbage Truck Accident Lawyer

Garbage trucks are among the most dangerous vehicles on residential streets. These heavy trucks make frequent stops, back up repeatedly, and operate with workers hanging off the back. The combination creates serious risks for pedestrians, cyclists, and other drivers.

Whether the garbage truck is operated by your city or a private waste management company, you have the right to compensation for your injuries. Our attorneys handle claims against both municipal and private operators.

Types of Garbage Truck Accident Cases

Backing Accidents

Garbage trucks backing up have limited visibility and can crush pedestrians and vehicles.

Pedestrian Strikes

Workers and bystanders struck by moving garbage trucks in residential areas.

Rear-End Collisions

Vehicles hitting stopped garbage trucks or trucks rear-ending stopped traffic.

Falling Debris Injuries

Trash and debris falling from trucks onto following vehicles.

Cyclist Accidents

Cyclists struck by garbage trucks making turns or pulling to the curb.

Child Injuries

Children struck in neighborhoods where garbage trucks operate during school hours.

Proving Liability in Garbage Truck Accident Cases

Establishing liability in truck accident cases requires thorough investigation and understanding of federal trucking regulations. Key elements we examine include:

  • Driver Negligence: Speeding, distracted driving, fatigue, or impairment
  • Company Negligence: Inadequate training, pressure to violate hours of service, poor maintenance
  • Regulatory Violations: FMCSA violations, overweight loads, improper cargo securement
  • Equipment Failures: Defective brakes, tires, or other mechanical components

Compensation for Garbage Truck Accident Victims

Truck accident victims may be entitled to significant compensation including:

Medical Expenses

Emergency care, surgeries, rehabilitation, and ongoing treatment costs

Lost Income

Current and future lost wages, reduced earning capacity

Pain & Suffering

Physical pain, emotional distress, loss of enjoyment of life

Punitive Damages

Additional damages when trucking companies act with gross negligence

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I sue the city for a garbage truck accident?

Yes, but claims against government entities have special notice requirements and shorter deadlines. Contact an attorney immediately to protect your rights.

What if the garbage truck was privately operated?

Private waste management companies like Waste Management and Republic Services can be sued like any other company.

How do I prove the garbage truck driver was negligent?

Evidence may include witness statements, surveillance footage, the trucks GPS data, and the drivers training records.

What are common causes of garbage truck accidents?

Common causes include driver fatigue from early morning shifts, blind spots, rushing to complete routes, and inadequate training.
