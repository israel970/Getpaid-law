The Unique Dangers for West Texas Motorcyclists

West Texas offers some of the most scenic riding in the state, but the Permian Basin oil boom has transformed these roads into some of the most dangerous for motorcyclists anywhere in America. Understanding these unique risks is essential to your case.

Oilfield Truck Traffic

The single biggest danger for West Texas motorcyclists is the constant stream of commercial oilfield vehicles:

  • Water haulers and sand trucks — Massive vehicles that may not see motorcycles in their blind spots
  • Equipment transports — Oversized loads that take up more than their lane
  • Fatigued drivers — 24/7 operations mean exhausted truckers sharing your road
  • Road debris — Cargo spills, gravel, and equipment falling from trucks create hazards
  • Damaged roads — Heavy truck traffic has destroyed road surfaces, creating dangerous conditions for two-wheelers

When an oilfield truck hits a motorcycle, the results are usually catastrophic. But these cases also often result in larger settlements due to commercial insurance policies and multiple liable parties.

Extreme Weather Hazards

West Texas weather creates additional dangers for motorcyclists:

  • Dust storms (haboobs) — Sudden zero-visibility conditions that leave riders nowhere to go
  • High winds — Gusts that can push motorcycles across lanes or into oncoming traffic
  • Extreme heat — Summer temperatures that can cause heat exhaustion and impaired judgment
  • Sand and gravel — Loose surfaces on roads and shoulders that cause loss of traction
  • Flash floods — Sudden water across roads, especially at low-water crossings

Remote Highways, Long Response Times

West Texas motorcycle accidents are often more severe because of the distances involved:

  • Emergency response times can exceed 30 minutes in remote areas
  • The nearest trauma center may be hours away by ground
  • Helicopter transport to Lubbock, El Paso, or Dallas is often necessary
  • These delays can turn survivable injuries into fatal ones

Types of Motorcycle Accidents We Handle

Our network handles all types of motorcycle crashes across West Texas and the Permian Basin.

Oilfield Truck Collisions

Crashes with water haulers, sand trucks, and equipment transports. Often catastrophic injuries with multiple liable parties.

Left-Turn Accidents

Drivers turning left in front of oncoming motorcycles. The #1 cause of fatal motorcycle accidents.

Rear-End Collisions

Distracted or fatigued drivers slamming into stopped or slowing motorcycles at intersections or in traffic.

Lane Change Accidents

Vehicles merging into motorcycles in their blind spots. Common with large trucks on highways.

Road Hazard Crashes

Accidents caused by potholes, gravel, cargo spills, and debris from oilfield trucks—hazards cars barely notice but can be deadly for bikes.

Intersection Collisions

T-bone accidents at rural intersections where drivers run stop signs or fail to yield to motorcycles.

Highway Crashes

High-speed accidents on I-20, I-10, US-285, and other major corridors through the Permian Basin.

Hit and Run

Drivers who strike motorcyclists and flee. Remote locations and limited witnesses make these tragically common.

Wind-Related Accidents

Crashes caused by sudden gusts pushing bikes into other lanes or off the road entirely.

Common Motorcycle Accident Injuries

Without the protection of a vehicle frame, motorcyclists suffer more severe injuries than car occupants in similar crashes:

  • Traumatic brain injuries — Even with a helmet, the force of impact can cause severe brain damage
  • Spinal cord injuries — Can result in partial or complete paralysis
  • Road rash — Severe skin damage requiring multiple surgeries and skin grafts
  • Multiple fractures — Broken legs, arms, pelvis, and ribs are common
  • Internal injuries — Organ damage and internal bleeding
  • Amputations — Loss of limbs from crushing injuries or surgical necessity
  • Facial injuries — Disfigurement requiring reconstructive surgery

Fighting Insurance Company Bias

Insurance companies often try to blame motorcyclists for their own injuries, claiming they were speeding, weaving through traffic, or being reckless—even when the other driver was clearly at fault. They also try to reduce compensation by arguing that riders "assumed the risk" by choosing to ride a motorcycle.

An experienced motorcycle accident attorney knows how to fight these tactics and prove that the other driver's negligence caused your injuries.

The Helmet Defense in Texas

Texas allows riders 21 and older to ride without helmets under certain conditions. But if you weren't wearing a helmet and suffered a head injury, insurance companies will argue your compensation should be reduced. This is called "comparative negligence," and Texas law allows it even though not wearing a helmet is legal.

An experienced attorney can argue that the head injury would have occurred regardless of helmet use, or that the helmet wouldn't have prevented the specific type of injury you sustained.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why are motorcycle accidents so dangerous in West Texas?
West Texas presents unique dangers for motorcyclists: heavy oilfield truck traffic that can't see bikes, extreme weather including high winds and dust storms, long distances from trauma centers, high speed limits, and poor road conditions from heavy commercial traffic. These factors make motorcycle accidents especially severe.
What if I was hit by an oilfield truck while riding my motorcycle?
Motorcycle accidents involving oilfield trucks often result in catastrophic or fatal injuries. Multiple parties may be liable, including the truck driver, trucking company, and oil company. Commercial trucks carry higher insurance limits, and these cases often result in larger settlements than typical motorcycle accidents.
Does Texas require motorcycle helmets?
Texas allows riders 21 and older to ride without a helmet if they have completed a safety course or carry at least $10,000 in medical insurance. However, not wearing a helmet can reduce your compensation if you suffer a head injury, as insurance companies may argue you contributed to your own injuries.
How much is a West Texas motorcycle accident case worth?
Motorcycle accident cases typically result in higher settlements than car accidents because injuries are more severe. Factors affecting value include the severity of injuries, medical costs, lost wages, whether an oilfield vehicle was involved, and the available insurance coverage. Cases involving oilfield trucks can be worth significantly more.
What should I do after a motorcycle accident in West Texas?
Call 911 immediately—remember that response times may be long in remote areas. Don't remove your helmet unless you must. Document the scene if you can. Get medical attention even if injuries seem minor. Do not give statements to insurance companies. Contact a motorcycle accident attorney before accepting any settlement.
