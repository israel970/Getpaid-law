Types of Kyle Car Accident Cases We Handle

Our network of car accident attorneys handles all types of cases throughout Kyle and Hays County.

Rear-End Collisions

The most common type of accident in traffic. Even "minor" rear-end crashes can cause whiplash, herniated discs, and long-term neck and back problems.

T-Bone Accidents

Side-impact crashes at intersections can cause catastrophic injuries. Drivers who run red lights or fail to yield are typically liable.

Head-On Collisions

The most dangerous type of crash. Often caused by wrong-way drivers, distracted driving, or drivers crossing the center line.

Hit and Run Accidents

If a driver fled the scene, you may still have options. Your UM/UIM coverage can provide compensation while police search for the driver.

Drunk drivers can be held liable for both compensatory and punitive damages. DUI accidents can result in significant settlements.

Multiple liable parties and insurance companies make these cases complex—but often more valuable.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a car accident lawyer cost?
Most car accident lawyers work on contingency, meaning you pay nothing upfront. The attorney only gets paid if you win your case, typically 33% of your settlement.
How long do I have to file a car accident claim in Texas?
In Texas, you have 2 years from the date of the car accident to file a personal injury lawsuit. Contact an attorney as soon as possible.
What should I do immediately after a car accident?
Call 911, seek medical attention, take photos, exchange information with other drivers but don't admit fault, and contact a car accident lawyer before giving recorded statements.
Can I still recover damages if I was partially at fault?
Yes. Texas follows a 'modified comparative fault' rule. You can recover damages as long as you're not more than 50% at fault.
