Why You Need a Toxic Exposure Lawyer

Workplace exposure to toxic chemicals can cause cancer, lung disease, neurological damage, and other serious illnesses. These conditions often develop years or decades after exposure, making it critical to act quickly once diagnosed.

Employers must protect workers from hazardous substances through engineering controls, protective equipment, and proper training. When they fail, victims can pursue compensation through both workers comp and third-party claims.

Types of Toxic Exposure Cases

Asbestos Exposure

Exposure to asbestos fibers causing mesothelioma, lung cancer, and asbestosis.

Silica Exposure

Crystalline silica dust causing silicosis and lung cancer in construction and manufacturing.

Chemical Burns

Acute exposure to caustic chemicals causing severe skin and respiratory burns.

Lead Poisoning

Chronic lead exposure causing neurological damage, kidney disease, and other conditions.

Solvent Exposure

Long-term exposure to industrial solvents causing liver damage and neurological problems.

Pesticide Exposure

Agricultural workers exposed to toxic pesticides and herbicides.

Proving Liability in Toxic Exposure Cases

Establishing liability in workplace injury cases requires thorough investigation and understanding of OSHA regulations and industry standards. Key elements we examine include:

  • Employer Negligence: Failure to provide safe working conditions, proper training, or required safety equipment
  • OSHA Violations: Documented safety violations that contributed to your injury
  • Equipment Defects: Faulty machinery or tools that caused or contributed to the accident
  • Third-Party Liability: Contractors, property owners, or manufacturers who share responsibility

Compensation for Toxic Exposure Victims

Workplace injury victims may be entitled to compensation beyond workers compensation including:

Medical Expenses

Emergency care, surgeries, rehabilitation, and ongoing treatment costs

Lost Income

Current and future lost wages, reduced earning capacity

Pain & Suffering

Physical pain, emotional distress, loss of enjoyment of life

Permanent Disability

Compensation for lasting impairments affecting your ability to work and live

Frequently Asked Questions

How long do I have to file a toxic exposure claim?

Statutes of limitations for occupational diseases often begin when you discover your illness, not when exposure occurred. Contact an attorney immediately.

Can I sue for asbestos exposure?

Yes, asbestos manufacturers, distributors, and employers who knew of the dangers can be held liable for mesothelioma and other asbestos diseases.

What is OSHA doing about toxic exposure?

OSHA sets permissible exposure limits for hundreds of chemicals and requires employers to monitor exposure and protect workers.

What compensation is available?

Victims may recover medical expenses, lost wages, pain and suffering, and in some cases access asbestos trust funds.
