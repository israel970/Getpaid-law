Why You Need a Workplace Vehicle Accident Lawyer

Vehicles in the workplace - forklifts, trucks, loaders, and other industrial equipment - cause thousands of serious injuries and deaths each year. Workers are struck, run over, crushed, and pinned by these heavy machines.

Employers must maintain safe traffic patterns, train operators properly, and ensure vehicles are in good working condition. When they fail, injured workers deserve full compensation.

Types of Workplace Vehicle Accident Cases

Forklift Accidents

Workers struck by, run over by, or crushed between forklifts and other objects.

Loading Dock Accidents

Injuries from trucks at loading docks, including struck-by and caught-between accidents.

Backing Accidents

Workers struck by vehicles backing up without proper spotters or warning systems.

Tip-Over Accidents

Workers crushed when forklifts, loaders, or other vehicles tip over.

Pedestrian Strikes

Workers on foot struck by moving vehicles in warehouses and job sites.

Maintenance Accidents

Workers injured while servicing or repairing workplace vehicles.

Proving Liability in Workplace Vehicle Accident Cases

Establishing liability in workplace injury cases requires thorough investigation and understanding of OSHA regulations and industry standards. Key elements we examine include:

  • Employer Negligence: Failure to provide safe working conditions, proper training, or required safety equipment
  • OSHA Violations: Documented safety violations that contributed to your injury
  • Equipment Defects: Faulty machinery or tools that caused or contributed to the accident
  • Third-Party Liability: Contractors, property owners, or manufacturers who share responsibility

Compensation for Workplace Vehicle Accident Victims

Workplace injury victims may be entitled to compensation beyond workers compensation including:

Medical Expenses

Emergency care, surgeries, rehabilitation, and ongoing treatment costs

Lost Income

Current and future lost wages, reduced earning capacity

Pain & Suffering

Physical pain, emotional distress, loss of enjoyment of life

Permanent Disability

Compensation for lasting impairments affecting your ability to work and live

Frequently Asked Questions

What causes workplace vehicle accidents?

Common causes include operator error, lack of training, poor visibility, speeding, mechanical failures, and lack of pedestrian safety measures.

Who is liable for a forklift accident?

Employers, vehicle operators, maintenance companies, and forklift manufacturers may all share liability.

What OSHA regulations apply?

OSHA regulates powered industrial trucks including operator training, maintenance requirements, and safe operating procedures.

Can I sue if I was the vehicle operator?

You may have claims against vehicle manufacturers, maintenance providers, or other parties even if you were operating the vehicle.
