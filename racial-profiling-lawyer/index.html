Understanding Racial Profiling Claims

Racial profiling violates the Equal Protection Clause of the Fourteenth Amendment. When police target you for stops, searches, or arrests based on your race rather than evidence of criminal activity, they violate your civil rights.

These cases require demonstrating that race was a motivating factor in police conduct. Our attorneys have experience building these claims using statistical evidence, pattern evidence, and direct proof of discriminatory intent.

Types of Racial Profiling Cases

Discriminatory Traffic Stops

Being pulled over because of your race rather than traffic violations.

Stop and Frisk Targeting

Being stopped and searched based on racial stereotypes.

Selective Enforcement

Laws enforced more aggressively against certain racial groups.

Store Following

Being followed by police or security in stores based on race.

Neighborhood Profiling

Being questioned for being in certain neighborhoods.

Pretextual Stops

Minor violations used as pretext to investigate based on race.

Filing a Racial Profiling Lawsuit

Civil rights claims against police are brought under 42 U.S.C. ยง 1983, which allows victims to sue government officials who violate constitutional rights. Key elements include:

  • Constitutional Violation: Proving the officer violated a specific constitutional right
  • Acting Under Color of Law: The officer was acting in their official capacity
  • Qualified Immunity: Overcoming the officers defense that their conduct was not clearly unconstitutional
  • Municipal Liability: Potentially holding the department liable for patterns of misconduct

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I prove racial profiling?

Evidence can include statistical data showing disparate treatment, similar incidents involving the same officers, statements by officers, and comparison to how others were treated.

What is the difference between racial profiling and legitimate policing?

Legitimate policing is based on specific evidence of criminal activity. Racial profiling relies on race as a factor in suspecting someone.

Can I sue for racial profiling?

Yes, you can bring claims under 42 U.S.C. ยง 1983 for violation of your equal protection rights under the Fourteenth Amendment.

What damages are available?

Victims can recover compensatory damages, emotional distress damages, and potentially punitive damages for intentional discrimination.
