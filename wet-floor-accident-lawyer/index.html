Understanding Wet Floor Accident Cases

Wet floor accidents are among the most common causes of serious injuries in stores, restaurants, and other businesses. Property owners and managers have a duty to either clean up spills promptly or warn customers of the hazard.

When businesses fail to maintain safe conditions, victims can suffer broken bones, head injuries, and back injuries that require extensive medical treatment. Our attorneys hold negligent property owners accountable.

Types of Wet Floor Accident Cases

Grocery Store Spills

Falls caused by spilled liquids, broken jars, and produce in grocery aisles.

Restaurant Floors

Slips on wet floors from drink spills, grease, and kitchen overflow.

Mopped Floors

Falls on recently mopped floors without proper warning signs.

Restroom Accidents

Slips on wet bathroom floors in public facilities.

Entrance Mat Hazards

Falls on wet or bunched entrance mats during rain or snow.

Pool Area Falls

Slips on wet pool decks and locker rooms without proper drainage.

Proving Negligence in Wet Floor Accident Cases

Premises liability cases require proving the property owner was negligent. Key elements include:

  • Duty of Care: The property owner owed you a duty to maintain safe premises
  • Breach of Duty: The owner failed to address or warn of the hazardous condition
  • Notice: The owner knew or should have known about the dangerous condition
  • Causation: The hazard directly caused your fall and resulting injuries

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I prove the business was negligent?

You must show the business knew or should have known about the wet floor and failed to clean it up or warn customers. Evidence includes surveillance footage, witness statements, and maintenance logs.

What if there was a wet floor sign?

A wet floor sign does not automatically relieve the business of liability. The sign must be visible and the floor must be cleaned within a reasonable time.

What compensation can I recover?

Victims can recover medical expenses, lost wages, pain and suffering, and other damages depending on the severity of injuries.

How long do I have to file a claim?

Statutes of limitations vary by state. Contact an attorney promptly to preserve evidence and protect your rights.
