Understanding Internal Injuries Cases

Internal injuries are among the most dangerous accident injuries because they may not be immediately apparent. Internal bleeding, organ damage, and punctured lungs can be fatal if not diagnosed and treated quickly.

The delayed nature of internal injury symptoms means victims may not realize the severity of their condition until its too late. Our attorneys help victims recover compensation for these serious injuries.

Types of Internal Injuries Cases

Internal Bleeding

Hemorrhaging from damaged blood vessels that can be life-threatening.

Organ Damage

Injuries to the liver, spleen, kidneys, or other internal organs.

Punctured Lung

Pneumothorax from broken ribs or blunt force trauma.

Abdominal Trauma

Injuries to the stomach, intestines, and other abdominal organs.

Cardiac Contusion

Bruising of the heart from chest impact.

Aortic Tear

Life-threatening damage to the bodys main artery.

Compensation for Internal Injuries Victims

Frequently Asked Questions

Why are internal injuries so dangerous?

Internal injuries may not show symptoms immediately. By the time symptoms appear, significant blood loss or organ damage may have occurred.

What are signs of internal bleeding?

Symptoms include abdominal pain, dizziness, fainting, bruising, blood in urine or stool, and rapid heart rate.

Should I see a doctor even if I feel fine after an accident?

Absolutely. Internal injuries can be present without immediate symptoms. Always get checked after any significant accident.

What compensation is available for internal injuries?

Victims can recover medical expenses, emergency surgery costs, lost wages, pain and suffering, and long-term care costs.
