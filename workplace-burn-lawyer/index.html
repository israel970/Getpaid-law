Why You Need a Workplace Burns & Explosions Lawyer

Workplace fires and explosions cause some of the most devastating injuries imaginable. Severe burns require months of painful treatment, multiple surgeries, and leave permanent disfigurement. Explosion injuries often include traumatic brain injury and hearing loss.

Many workplace fires and explosions are preventable. When employers fail to control ignition sources, properly store flammable materials, or maintain safe equipment, they can be held liable for resulting injuries.

Types of Workplace Burns & Explosions Cases

Refinery Explosions

Catastrophic explosions at oil refineries and chemical plants.

Chemical Fires

Fires from improperly stored or handled flammable chemicals.

Electrical Fires

Fires caused by faulty wiring, overloaded circuits, or arc flash events.

Welding Accidents

Burns and fires from welding operations and cutting torches.

Steam Burns

Scalding injuries from steam leaks and pressure vessel failures.

Gas Explosions

Explosions from natural gas leaks, propane, or other fuel gases.

Proving Liability in Workplace Burns & Explosions Cases

Establishing liability in workplace injury cases requires thorough investigation and understanding of OSHA regulations and industry standards. Key elements we examine include:

  • Employer Negligence: Failure to provide safe working conditions, proper training, or required safety equipment
  • OSHA Violations: Documented safety violations that contributed to your injury
  • Equipment Defects: Faulty machinery or tools that caused or contributed to the accident
  • Third-Party Liability: Contractors, property owners, or manufacturers who share responsibility

Frequently Asked Questions

What types of burn injuries are most serious?

Third-degree burns destroy all layers of skin and underlying tissue. Large surface area burns are life-threatening and require specialized treatment.

Who is liable for workplace explosions?

Employers, equipment manufacturers, chemical suppliers, and contractors may all share liability for explosion injuries.

What is the treatment for severe burns?

Severe burns require specialized burn centers, skin grafts, reconstructive surgery, and extensive rehabilitation. Lifetime care costs can reach millions.

Can I receive compensation for scarring and disfigurement?

Yes, burn victims can recover substantial compensation for permanent scarring, disfigurement, and the emotional trauma they cause.
