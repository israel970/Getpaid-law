Why You Need a Fall from Height Lawyer

Falls from height are the leading cause of death in the construction industry and cause thousands of severe injuries every year. Even falls from relatively low heights can result in traumatic brain injuries, spinal cord damage, and broken bones.

OSHA requires fall protection for workers at heights of six feet or more in construction. When employers fail to provide guardrails, safety nets, or personal fall arrest systems, they can be held liable for resulting injuries.

Types of Fall from Height Cases

Ladder Falls

Falls from improperly secured, defective, or incorrectly positioned ladders.

Scaffold Falls

Falls from scaffolding due to missing guardrails, unstable platforms, or improper assembly.

Roof Falls

Falls through skylights, roof openings, or off roof edges without proper protection.

Elevated Platform Falls

Falls from aerial lifts, boom lifts, and scissor lifts.

Floor Opening Falls

Falls through unguarded floor holes, hatchways, and stairwell openings.

Structural Falls

Falls from steel beams, girders, and structural elements during erection.

Proving Liability in Fall from Height Cases

Establishing liability in workplace injury cases requires thorough investigation and understanding of OSHA regulations and industry standards. Key elements we examine include:

  • Employer Negligence: Failure to provide safe working conditions, proper training, or required safety equipment
  • OSHA Violations: Documented safety violations that contributed to your injury
  • Equipment Defects: Faulty machinery or tools that caused or contributed to the accident
  • Third-Party Liability: Contractors, property owners, or manufacturers who share responsibility

Compensation for Fall from Height Victims

Workplace injury victims may be entitled to compensation beyond workers compensation including:

Medical Expenses

Emergency care, surgeries, rehabilitation, and ongoing treatment costs

Lost Income

Current and future lost wages, reduced earning capacity

Pain & Suffering

Physical pain, emotional distress, loss of enjoyment of life

Permanent Disability

Compensation for lasting impairments affecting your ability to work and live

Frequently Asked Questions

What safety equipment should have prevented my fall?

Depending on the situation: guardrails, safety nets, personal fall arrest systems, warning lines, or controlled access zones.

Who is liable for a fall from height?

Potentially liable parties include general contractors, subcontractors, property owners, equipment manufacturers, and safety coordinators.

What injuries are common in falls from height?

Traumatic brain injuries, spinal cord injuries, broken bones, internal bleeding, and fatalities are common.

Can I sue if I was partially at fault?

In many states, you can still recover compensation even if partially at fault, though your recovery may be reduced.
